\abstract{The contribution of our work is to address multi-word entity (MWEntity) recognition in a highly multilingual environment. The first part of this chapter describes completed work on recognising MWEntities in large volumes of text in 22 different languages, on identifying monolingual variants for the same entity and on linking the equivalent groups of variants across all languages. The second part describes ongoing work on learning MWEntity recognition rules based on the already recognised MWEntities, using distributional patterns. This rule-learning approach, which has currently been tested only for English and Spanish, does not require linguistic tools such as part-of-speech taggers so that it is perfectly suitable for our highly multilingual environment. When adding these learnt patterns to an existing rule-based Named Entity Recognition (NER) system, F1 performance for Spanish increases from 42.4\% to 50\% (18\% increase) and for English from 43.4\% to 44.5\% (2.5\% increase). The purpose of our effort is to improve on current methods for Named Entity Recognition (NER) in order to turn free text into semi-structured data that can be used for improved search, for linking related news over time and across languages, for trend detection and – more generally – for a more advanced intelligent analysis of large volumes of multilingual text collections.}

\ili{}\subsubsection\ili{}{Aggregation\ili{} strategies}\ili{}
\ili{}\label\ili{}{aggregation\ili{} clusters}\ili{}
\ili{}
\ili{} \ili{}
We\ili{} formulate\ili{} cluster\ili{} linking\ili{} as\ili{} the\ili{} task\ili{} of\ili{} identifying\ili{} connected\ili{}
components\ili{} in\ili{} a\ili{} graph\ili{},\ili{} where\ili{} monolingual\ili{} clusters\ili{} are\ili{} vertices\ili{} and\ili{}
where\ili{} edges\ili{} represent\ili{} links\ili{} of\ili{} related\ili{} clusters\ili{} across\ili{}
languages\ili{}.\ili{} Clusters\ili{} are\ili{} linked\ili{} if\ili{} their\ili{} similarity\ili{} is\ili{} above\ili{} a\ili{} certain\ili{}
threshold\ili{} \ili{}$\ili{}\alpha\ili{}$\ili{}.\ili{} During\ili{} preliminary\ili{} experiments\ili{},\ili{} we\ili{} had\ili{} also\ili{} tested\ili{}
\ili{}`pure\ili{}'\ili{} clustering\ili{} algorithms\ili{},\ili{} but\ili{} it\ili{} turned\ili{} out\ili{} that\ili{} the\ili{} graph\ili{}
approach\ili{} was\ili{} more\ili{} efficient\ili{}.\ili{}
\ili{}
For\ili{} the\ili{} last\ili{} two\ili{} cluster\ili{} aggregation\ili{} methods\ili{} \ili{}(\ili{}\textit\ili{}{TokAgg}\ili{} and\ili{}
\ili{}\textit\ili{}{TransTokAgg}\ili{})\ili{},\ili{} we\ili{} applied\ili{} two\ili{} similarity\ili{} measures\ili{}:\ili{} cosine\ili{} and\ili{}
ComMNZ\ili{}.\ili{} The\ili{} latter\ili{} is\ili{} actually\ili{} a\ili{} data\ili{} fusion\ili{} algorithm\ili{} \ili{}\citep\ili{}{Fox\ili{}-94}\ili{}
which\ili{} we\ili{} assimilate\ili{},\ili{} in\ili{} this\ili{} context\ili{},\ili{} to\ili{} a\ili{} similarity\ili{} measure\ili{}.\ili{} \ili{} This\ili{}
algorithm\ili{} aims\ili{} at\ili{} measuring\ili{} the\ili{} similarity\ili{} between\ili{} two\ili{} objects\ili{} having\ili{}
multiple\ili{} comparison\ili{} criteria\ili{}.\ili{} Specifically\ili{},\ili{} the\ili{} overall\ili{} similarity\ili{}
score\ili{} between\ili{} two\ili{} objects\ili{} is\ili{} better\ili{} when\ili{} those\ili{} objects\ili{} have\ili{} reasonable\ili{}
similarity\ili{} scores\ili{} for\ili{} all\ili{} criteria\ili{} than\ili{} when\ili{} they\ili{} have\ili{} a\ili{} very\ili{} good\ili{}
similarity\ili{} score\ili{} for\ili{} one\ili{} criterion\ili{},\ili{} and\ili{} less\ili{} good\ili{} or\ili{} no\ili{} value\ili{} for\ili{} the\ili{}
others\ili{}.\ili{} \ili{} In\ili{} our\ili{} case\ili{},\ili{} it\ili{} would\ili{} promote\ili{} the\ili{} similarity\ili{} between\ili{} two\ili{}
clusters\ili{} \ili{}$c_i\ili{}$\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}$c_j\ili{}$\ili{} if\ili{} they\ili{} have\ili{} many\ili{} similar\ili{} or\ili{} translated\ili{}
tokens\ili{} \ili{}$t_k\ili{}$\ili{} with\ili{} a\ili{} reasonable\ili{} similarity\ili{} score\ili{},\ili{} and\ili{} it\ili{} would\ili{} decrease\ili{}
the\ili{} similarity\ili{} between\ili{} two\ili{} clusters\ili{} \ili{}$c_i\ili{}$\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}$c_j\ili{}$\ili{} if\ili{} they\ili{} have\ili{} few\ili{}
similar\ili{} or\ili{} translated\ili{} tokens\ili{} \ili{}$t_k\ili{}$\ili{} with\ili{} a\ili{} very\ili{} high\ili{} similarity\ili{} score\ili{}:\ili{}
\ili{}
\ili{}\begin\ili{}{equation}\ili{}
CombMNZ\ili{}(c_i\ili{},c_j\ili{})\ili{} \ili{}=\ili{} \ili{}\sum_\ili{}{t_k\ili{}\in\ili{} c_j}\ili{} \ili{}\frac\ili{}{C\ili{}(c_i\ili{},t_k\ili{})}\ili{}{\ili{}\sum_\ili{}{t_l\ili{}\in\ili{}
\ili{} \ili{} \ili{} \ili{} c_i}\ili{} C\ili{}(c_i\ili{},t_l\ili{})}\ili{} \ili{}\times\ili{} \ili{}\sum_\ili{}{t_k\ili{}\in\ili{} c_j}\ili{} 1_\ili{}{\ili{}\\ili{}{C\ili{}(c_i\ili{},t_k\ili{})\ili{}\neq0\ili{}\}}\ili{}
\ili{}\end\ili{}{equation}\ili{}

\ili{}\subsubsection\ili{}{Evaluation\ili{} dataset}\ili{}
\ili{}\label\ili{}{evalData}\ili{}
\ili{}
As\ili{} described\ili{} in\ili{} Section\ili{} \ili{}\ref\ili{}{starting}\ili{},\ili{} the\ili{} starting\ili{} point\ili{} of\ili{} our\ili{}
experiments\ili{} is\ili{} a\ili{} set\ili{} of\ili{} 81\ili{},000\ili{} monolingual\ili{} clusters\ili{} with\ili{} one\ili{} acronym\ili{}

\ili{}\subsubsection\ili{}{Parameters}\ili{}
\ili{}\label\ili{}{settings}\ili{}
\ili{}
Parameters\ili{} have\ili{} to\ili{} be\ili{} set\ili{} with\ili{} regards\ili{} to\ili{},\ili{} first\ili{},\ili{} the\ili{} thresholds\ili{}
\ili{}$\ili{}\delta\ili{}$\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}$\ili{}\beta\ili{}$\ili{} applied\ili{} to\ili{} filter\ili{} out\ili{} some\ili{} similarity\ili{} values\ili{} in\ili{}
the\ili{} above\ili{}-mentioned\ili{} token\ili{} matrices\ili{} \ili{}(\ili{}$C\ili{}\_Tok_\ili{}{l}\ili{}$\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}$C\ili{}\_TransTok_\ili{}{l}\ili{}$\ili{})\ili{} and\ili{},\ili{} second\ili{},\ili{}
the\ili{} threshold\ili{} \ili{}$\ili{}\alpha\ili{}$\ili{} applied\ili{} to\ili{} the\ili{} aggregation\ili{} strategies\ili{},\ili{} i\ili{}.e\ili{}.\ili{} the\ili{}
one\ili{} above\ili{} which\ili{} clusters\ili{} are\ili{} aggregated\ili{}.\ili{}
\ili{}

\ili{}$C\ili{}\_Tok_\ili{}{l}\ili{}$\ili{},\ili{} the\ili{} threshold\ili{} \ili{}$\ili{}\delta\ili{}$\ili{} should\ili{} be\ili{} high\ili{} in\ili{} order\ili{} to\ili{}

level\ili{}.\ili{} For\ili{} our\ili{} experiments\ili{},\ili{} the\ili{} values\ili{} of\ili{} \ili{}$\ili{}\delta\ili{}$\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}$\ili{}\beta\ili{}$\ili{} were\ili{}

Cluster\ili{} aggregation\ili{} is\ili{} allowed\ili{} when\ili{} the\ili{} cluster\ili{} similarity\ili{} \ili{}(either\ili{} in\ili{} terms\ili{} of\ili{} cosine\ili{} or\ili{}
CombMNZ\ili{})\ili{} is\ili{} above\ili{} a\ili{} certain\ili{} threshold\ili{} \ili{}$\ili{}\alpha\ili{}$\ili{}.\ili{} We\ili{} experimented\ili{} with\ili{}
different\ili{} values\ili{} for\ili{} \ili{}$\ili{}\alpha\ili{}$\ili{},\ili{} ranging\ili{} from\ili{} 0\ili{}.7\ili{} to\ili{} 1\ili{} \ili{}(cf\ili{}.\ili{} Section\ili{}
\ili{}\ref\ili{}{results}\ili{})\ili{}.\ili{}
\ili{}

following\ili{} constraints\ili{}:\ili{} two\ili{} clusters\ili{} \ili{}$c_1\ili{}$\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}$c_2\ili{}$\ili{} are\ili{} linked\ili{} if\ili{}
their\ili{} similarity\ili{} is\ili{} above\ili{} \ili{}$\ili{}\alpha\ili{}$\ili{} and\ili{} if\ili{} \ili{}$c_1\ili{}$\ili{} is\ili{} in\ili{} the\ili{} \ili{}$k\ili{}$\ili{} most\ili{}

case\ili{} for\ili{} short\ili{} and\ili{} frequent\ili{} expansions\ili{},\ili{} e\ili{}.g\ili{}.\ili{} \ili{}\textit\ili{}{Olympic\ili{}
\ili{} \ili{} Committee}\ili{} which\ili{} is\ili{} highly\ili{} similar\ili{} to\ili{} a\ili{} cluster\ili{} containing\ili{}

clusters\ili{} containing\ili{} more\ili{} specific\ili{} expansions\ili{} such\ili{} as\ili{} \ili{}\textit\ili{}{Vancouver\ili{}
\ili{} \ili{} Olympic\ili{} Committee}\ili{}.\ili{} In\ili{} our\ili{} experiments\ili{},\ili{} \ili{}$k\ili{}$\ili{} equals\ili{} 3\ili{}.\ili{}

\ili{}\subsubsection\ili{}{Translation\ili{} models}\ili{}
\ili{}\label\ili{}{trans}\ili{}
Cluster\ili{} representations\ili{} based\ili{} on\ili{} translated\ili{} tokens\ili{} correspond\ili{} to\ili{}
lexical\ili{} conditional\ili{} translation\ili{} probabilities\ili{} computed\ili{} for\ili{} three\ili{}
language\ili{} pairs\ili{},\ili{} between\ili{} \ili{}\ili\ili{}{English}\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}\ili\ili{}{French}\ili{},\ili{} \ili{}\ili\ili{}{German}\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}\ili\ili{}{Italian}\ili{}.\ili{} The\ili{}
translation\ili{} models\ili{} were\ili{} trained\ili{} on\ili{} \ili{}\isi\ili{}{parallel\ili{} corpora}\ili{} built\ili{} from\ili{}

redirection\ili{} tables\ili{} \ili{}$rt_1\ili{}$\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}$rt_2\ili{}$\ili{} in\ili{} languages\ili{} \ili{}$l_1\ili{}$\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}$l_2\ili{}$\ili{},\ili{}

Section\ili{} \ili{}\ref\ili{}{evalData}\ili{})\ili{}.\ili{} As\ili{} a\ili{} consequence\ili{},\ili{} the\ili{} 1\ili{}.7\ili{} million\ili{} expansions\ili{}
and\ili{} 0\ili{}.4\ili{} million\ili{} acronyms\ili{} on\ili{} which\ili{} the\ili{} approach\ili{} is\ili{} applied\ili{} were\ili{} removed\ili{}

\ili{}\subsubsection\ili{}{Evaluation\ili{} measures}\ili{}
\ili{}

\ili{} C}\ili{}{EXP\ili{}(c\ili{})_\ili{}{false}}}\ili{}
\ili{}\end\ili{}{equation}\ili{}
\ili{}\begin\ili{}{equation}\ili{}
MAV\ili{}-R\ili{} \ili{}(C\ili{})\ili{} \ili{}=\ili{} \ili{}\frac\ili{}{\ili{}\sum_\ili{}{c\ili{} \ili{}\in\ili{} C}\ili{}{EXP\ili{}(c\ili{})_\ili{}{true}}}\ili{}{\ili{}\sum_\ili{}{c\ili{} \ili{}\in\ili{} C}\ili{}{EXP\ili{}(c\ili{})_\ili{}{true}}\ili{}+\ili{}\sum_\ili{}{c\ili{} \ili{}\in\ili{} C}\ili{}{EXP\ili{}(c\ili{})_\ili{}{miss}}}\ili{}
\ili{}\end\ili{}{equation}\ili{}

class\ili{} of\ili{} the\ili{} gold\ili{} standard\ili{},\ili{} and\ili{} \ili{}$EXP\ili{}(c\ili{})_\ili{}{false}\ili{}$\ili{} is\ili{} the\ili{} set\ili{} of\ili{}
expansions\ili{} in\ili{} a\ili{} cluster\ili{} \ili{}$c\ili{}$\ili{} which\ili{} do\ili{} not\ili{} appear\ili{} in\ili{} the\ili{} gold\ili{}
standard\ili{}\footnote\ili{}{We\ili{} tried\ili{} two\ili{} other\ili{} metrics\ili{}:\ili{} macro\ili{}-average\ili{} and\ili{}
\ili{} \ili{} B\ili{}-cubed\ili{} measure\ili{} \ili{}\citep\ili{}{Bagga\ili{}-98}\ili{} but\ili{} since\ili{} results\ili{} are\ili{} comparable\ili{} we\ili{}
\ili{} \ili{} do\ili{} not\ili{} report\ili{} them\ili{}.}\ili{}.\ili{}
